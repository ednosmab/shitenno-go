# 00 — VISION

> The Constitution of the Nexus System

## Manifesto

We believe that engineering teams deserve more than tools. They deserve a system that understands their context, learns from their decisions, and evolves with their maturity.

Nexus exists because the gap between **having code** and **governing code** is where projects go to die. Teams accumulate knowledge — ADRs, skills, workflows, contracts — but that knowledge lives in isolation. It doesn't connect. It doesn't learn. It doesn't adapt.

Nexus changes that.

## What is Nexus

Nexus is an **AI governance framework** that:

1. **Measures** project complexity through static and behavioral analysis
2. **Detects** patterns from history — recurring errors, reverted decisions, hot areas
3. **Audits** governance health — dead rules, missing docs, stale context
4. **Recommends** next actions based on current state, not generic advice
5. **Evolves** alongside the team — from junior to senior, from chaos to governance

Nexus is not a linter. It is not a CI pipeline. It is not a project manager.

Nexus is a **system that thinks about how you work**.

## What Problem Does Nexus Solve

Every engineering team faces the same fundamental problem: **knowledge decays**.

- ADRs are written but never referenced
- Skills are documented but never extracted from patterns
- Workflows are defined but never enforced
- Context is lost between sessions
- Decisions are repeated because no one remembers the previous one

This is **Knowledge Debt** — the silent killer of engineering productivity.

Nexus detects Knowledge Debt before it compounds. It connects the artifacts that should be connected. It recommends the knowledge that should exist but doesn't.

## Where Nexus is Going

Nexus evolves through three phases:

### Phase 1 — CLI Tool (Current)
A command-line tool that analyzes projects, scores complexity, detects patterns, and recommends actions. 10 commands, 13 core modules, 9 capabilities.

### Phase 2 — Adaptive System (Next)
An event-driven system where modules communicate, feedback loops exist, and the system learns from acceptance/rejection of recommendations. The CLI becomes an interface to a deeper engine.

### Phase 3 — Autonomous Governance (Future)
A system that governs itself. That detects its own knowledge gaps. That recommends its own evolution. That adapts its behavior based on team maturity without human intervention.

## What Nexus is NOT

- **Not a replacement for engineering judgment.** Nexus recommends. Humans decide.
- **Not a silver bullet.** Nexus amplifies good practices; it cannot fix bad ones.
- **Not a monitoring tool.** Nexus analyzes governance, not uptime.
- **Not a CI/CD pipeline.** Nexus complements CI; it does not replace it.
- **Not a project manager.** Nexus tracks knowledge, not tickets.

## The Name

**Nexus** means "a connection or series of connections linking two or more things."

That is exactly what this system does. It connects:

- Knowledge to decisions
- Decisions to implementations
- Implementations to evidence
- Evidence to evolution

Every artifact in Nexus — every ADR, skill, contract, workflow, runbook — is a node in a graph. The graph is the system. The system is the governance.

## The Promise

When Nexus is complete, any team — regardless of maturity — will be able to:

1. **Understand** their engineering state in real-time
2. **Govern** their knowledge without bureaucratic overhead
3. **Evolve** their practices based on evidence, not opinion
4. **Integrate** AI agents that respect their governance rules
5. **Scale** their engineering culture without losing coherence

This is not a tool. This is a system. And this document is its constitution.
